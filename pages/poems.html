The Dance of the Dragonflies

Is it buzzing electric
or waltzing wings
that dance across this clear-cut path?

It grows harder to tell
as the day drags on I am weary,

a mere servant to these woods,
so forgive me when I ask
why the sky so strangely open up
and the horizon so plainly show,

why the oaken and the maple which have I known;
the trusty pines,
crystallize steel at harsh right angle
not sprouting, driven
into the still weary soil.

why it is only the scrappy and fearless grasses
who call this home

The young and the bold come up here
with fire and sex in their eyes
summoning passions out of stale air
to drive away the night's cold,
heavy breaths of raw voltage
drawing charge up their desires.

I think I know a poison or two
which finds a way through hairline fractures,
minute cracks in the concrete
some flower which, distilled, is wiser than any mortal.

"What is this stone," the Earth cries out,
"for surely I did not forge it."
crumbles away and forgets..."
where once hid treasure, drives out gentle moles and humble worms,
and yet I mend.

-

And still, I return, drawn in by jolts of clarity;
fleeting visions of godhood
which I have known too well to forget.

I swore I would climb those wretched towers
if not for the view, to conquer
but each day they grow taller and I, wiser.

I think I hear it, if only faintly
in the distance,
across the empty space
cruelly cut through tapestries of delight.
The Dance of the Dragonflies has begun.
